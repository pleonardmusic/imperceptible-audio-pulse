Sound for Aging — Research Tool v3 (FFT)

ERB Comb
40Hz Processor

Two complementary ERB comb tracks, built with fast FFT frequency-domain masking. Track A has odd bands at full volume and even bands attenuated; Track B is the inverse. Each track is amplitude-modulated at 40Hz — so A and B alone already contain the entrainment envelope, and A + B reconstructs the full stimulus. Stereo in, stereo out.

02 — Pre-Processing
Compression0 = bypass, 1 = fully flatten dynamics
0.20

Flattens loudness variations so the 40Hz modulation sits on a more uniform carrier. Low values (0.1–0.3) gently even out dynamics and work for most music. Turn up (0.6–1.0) for highly rhythmic tracks where drum hits or silences would otherwise mask the entrainment envelope. At 1.0 expect pumping/breathing artifacts — fine for research stimulus, not for casual listening.

Off-band level is your efficacy/comfort tradeoff. Lower = stronger perceivable stimulus. 0.0–0.2 recommended for effectiveness. Above 0.5 may be imperceptible.

Band edge softness controls how sharply neighboring bands separate in frequency, as a % of each band's own ERB width (not a flat Hz amount — a flat amount would disproportionately blur the narrow bass bands while barely touching the wide treble bands). 0 = abrupt cut. Higher values taper the transition more, blurring the comb's on/off contrast. Current default (15%) keeps the on/off contrast relatively crisp while still softening the hardest edges of the comb transitions.

Min/Max Pulse Frequency tradeoff (2026-06-24 finding): raising Min Pulse Frequency away from full-range (originally 80Hz, now defaulted to 500Hz) makes the audio sound dramatically more normal — but it is NOT a free win neurally. The 40Hz auditory steady-state response (ASSR) literature shows response amplitude is largest for low carrier frequencies and decreases as carrier frequency rises (a 250Hz carrier gives ~3x the ASSR amplitude of a 4000Hz carrier). So excluding bass from the pulse likely trades away some real entrainment strength for a cleaner sound, not a pure improvement. There's no known setting that wins on both axes. Above ~5000Hz (Max Pulse Frequency), this tradeoff mostly disappears — there's no evidence entrainment depends on content that high, so excluding treble is closer to a free win. Full citations at the bottom of this page.

04 — Modulation Parameters
Mod FrequencyHz — entrainment target (integer)
40 Hz
Crossfade Shape0 = square, 1 = full Hanning — THE main control for gritty/static artifacts, see below
1.00

The same shape is applied to the AM envelopes of Track A and Track B individually, so their sum always reconstructs the full signal regardless of shape. Hanning (1) = smooth sinusoidal AM, no discontinuity. Square (0) = hard on/off switching every 1/40s — a sharp discontinuity that injects broadband harmonic energy on every cycle.

This is THE dominant cause of gritty/clicky/static-sounding output (2026-06-24 finding) — much more so than band edge softness or band count, which were investigated first and turned out to be minor by comparison. Listening-test result: gritty below ~50%, "bearable" around 50%, clean at 1.0. Keep this at or near 1.0 (the default) — there is little reason to lower it.

References
Background and full design-decision log: PROJECT_NOTES.md in this project folder.

40Hz gamma entrainment (the reason this app exists):
Iaccarino et al. 2016, Nature — 40Hz light flicker reduces amyloid-β via microglia in AD mouse models; effect is frequency-specific (20/80Hz/random did not work).
Martorell et al. 2019, Cell, "Multisensory Gamma Stimulation Ameliorates Alzheimer's-Associated Pathology" — combined 40Hz light+sound (not light or sound alone) reduced Aβ and improved cognition in mice. Auditory stimulus was 1ms clicks, 25ms intervals (i.e. 40Hz).
Jones et al. 2019, J. Alzheimer's Dis. (Galway/NUI) — 40Hz light flicker produces the strongest human EEG entrainment vs 60/80Hz; eyes-open >> eyes-closed.
Hajos et al. 2024, Frontiers in Neurology, OVERTURE trial (Cognito Therapeutics) — N=76 AD patients, 6mo 40Hz light+sound: missed primary composite endpoint, but significant secondary signals (MMSE, ADCS-ADL, brain volume preservation); no amyloid-PET change.

Masking the stimulus (the actual idea this app implements):
Carstensen et al. 2020, SPIE, "Invisible Spectral Flicker" (OptoCeutics/DTU) — alternates two chromaticity/luminance-matched LED spectra at 40Hz so the eye perceives constant light while the spectral composition still switches; this app is the audio analog (ERB comb instead of LED spectra).

Auditory steady-state response (carrier-frequency choice for Min/Max Pulse Frequency):
Frequency specificity of 40-Hz auditory steady-state responses — ASSR reliably measured for AM-tone carriers 250Hz-4000Hz; amplitude decreases as carrier frequency increases (250Hz ASSR ~3x larger than at 4000Hz). Used here to judge whether excluding bass (raising Min Pulse Frequency) trades away neural-entrainment strength, not just audible artifact.

ERB perceptual frequency scale:
Glasberg & Moore 1990, "Derivation of auditory filter shapes from notched-noise data" — basis for hzToErb/erbToHz in app.js.
